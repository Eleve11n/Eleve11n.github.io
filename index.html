Because Thailand has been a more famous country in the West while Cambodia was hidden in the midst of war and conflict, many elements that shaped these other peoples are unknown. For example, the Thai, Lao and other languages of the region have many roots in the older Khmer language. Likewise, pre-Angkorian and Angkorian architecture had a great influence on territories in present-day Thailand, Laos, southern Vietnam, Burma and even Malaysia. Southeast Asian martial arts such as Muay Thai (Thailand), Muay Lao (Laos), Lethwei (Burma) and others have a close origin or influence with the ancient Khmer fighting art known as Bokator (lion splitting) and its more modern expression Prodal Serei (wrestling).

Cambodia has greatly improved its tourism infrastructure and is now an easy country to visit, with a modern e-visa and visa-on-arrival service and a large investment in hotels. The people are very friendly, unassuming and each province has its attractions. As its majority religion is Theravada Buddhism, it shares a great deal of cultural similarity with neighbouring Thailand and Laos, as well as regional and cultural integration with southern Vietnam. In Thailand's Surin province and in the southern provinces of Vietnam (Kampuchi Kraom or Lower Cambodia), Khmer ethnic groups are closely related to their Cambodian brethren.

Top 3 Amazing Places

    2. Phnom Penh is the most populous city and capital of Cambodia since French colonisation. It is also the economic, industrial, political, cultural and diplomatic centre of the country. The metropolitan area is home to some 2.2 million inhabitants.

  2. Angkor Wat
    3.

    Angkor Wat , sometimes also written Angkor Vat, is the largest and also the best preserved of the Hindu temples that make up the Angkor settlement. It is considered to be the largest religious structure ever built, and one of the most important archaeological treasures in the world.

  3. Siem Reap
    4.

    Siem Reap is the capital of Siem Reap Province in Cambodia. It is located in the heart of the province, at the crossroads of the National Highway linking the capital, Phnom Penh, and Kompung Thom with Sisophon. From the town, the National Highway, which leads to the north of the country, starts from the town.

Animals

Cambodia is home to all kinds of animals, some of which are becoming increasingly rare, such as the Javan rhino, the Asian elephant, the Ko Prey, the wild ox, the water buffalo, the tiger and the sao la, an antelope. In the country's forests there are still bears, buffalo, leopards, monkeys and snakes.
Not forgetting the crocodiles, which, because of the interest in their meat and skin, are mainly raised on farms.
If you are a bird enthusiast, you must go to Cambodia, as many species live here. You can see cranes, airons, sparrowhawks, hawks, falcons, kites, eagles, vultures, peacocks, quails, woodcock, owls, herons, pelicans, wild ducks, cormorants.
